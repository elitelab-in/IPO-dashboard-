Sacrificing Short-Term Gains for Long-Term Wealth Creation 💸

Elitelab 15/06/2026 2 min read

One of the most lucrative, yet misunderstood, setups in the Indian stock market is the **IPO Base**. When a company goes public, there is often massive hype, followed by a period of cooling off where the smart money quietly accumulates shares. If you can spot the end of this accumulation phase, you position yourself for explosive returns.

In this guide, we will walk you through the anatomy of a perfect IPO base and exactly what technical indicators you should be looking for before you execute a trade.

The Anatomy of an IPO Base

An IPO base typically forms within the first few weeks or months after a stock lists. It is characterized by a significant decline from its listing day highs, followed by a period of sideways consolidation. This consolidation is the "base".

"The longer the base, the higher in space. A stock that consolidates for 12 weeks has significantly more stored energy than a stock that consolidates for 2."

Here are the three critical phases you must identify:

  • The Flush: The initial panic selling by retail investors who bought the hype.
  • The Dead Zone: Volume dries up significantly. Volatility contracts. This is where institutional players are accumulating without driving the price up.
  • The Right Side: Price starts making higher lows. Volume begins to swell on up days.

Volume is Your Best Indicator

Price action without volume is just noise. When analyzing an IPO base, you want to see a specific phenomenon known as "dry up" volume at the absolute bottom of the base. If you look at the daily charts, the red volume bars should be shrinking, indicating that selling pressure has completely exhausted itself.

Conversely, as the stock starts creeping up the right side of the base, you want to see "pocket pivots" — days where the buying volume is significantly higher than the largest selling volume in the past 10 days.

Conclusion

Trading IPO bases requires immense patience. You cannot anticipate the breakout; you must wait for the setup to prove itself.
